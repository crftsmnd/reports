# Global Heatwave Crisis — June 2026

**Briefing date:** June 30, 2026 
**Sources:** CNN, CBS News, ABC News, NYT, USA Today, AccuWeather, CNBC, Weather.com, Yale Climate Connections, Wikipedia, Independent, IMD, NDTV, News18, Down To Earth, India Water Portal

---

## 🌍 Overview

A series of simultaneous, record-shattering heatwaves are gripping three continents — Europe, North America, and Asia — in late June 2026. Each region is experiencing its own extreme heat event driven by persistent heat domes, with climate scientists warning this could be one of the most widespread heat events in modern record keeping.

---

## 🇪🇺 EUROPE — "Unprecedented June Heat"

Europe has been hit hardest and earliest. From late May 2026, a Saharan heat dome began pushing across the continent, and the heatwave peaked in the third week of June.

### Key Facts
- **Peak temperatures:** 44°C+ across Iberia, over 37.8°C in France and Spain
- **Countries breaking national records:** Belgium, France, Germany, Romania, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, Poland, Czech Republic, Denmark, Spain, UK, Switzerland
- **UK:** Hottest June day ever — 35.8°C (96.4°F) recorded in Wiggonholt, Sussex
- **Spain:** Warmest June days since at least 1950 — temperatures 7.1°C above average
- **France:** 6,000 schools closed or adapted; nuclear plant temporarily shut down because river water used for cooling became too warm
- **UK:** Unprecedented combination of record heat and Miami-level humidity in London
- **Red alerts** issued across UK, France, Spain, and Italy
- **Switzerland & Denmark** also smashed all-time June records

### Impacts
- Thousands of schools closed across the UK and France
- Train services slashed in the UK; passengers urged not to travel
- Nuclear power restrictions in France
- WHO appealed to world leaders to prioritize climate action

---

## 🇮🇳 INDIA — "98 of 100 Hottest Cities"

India has been enduring an extended heatwave that began in April 2026 and has intensified through June.

### Key Facts
- **98 of the world's 100 hottest cities** are currently in India
- **6 cities crossed 46°C** in April alone
- **Delhi and Rajasthan** facing temperatures above 45°C in June
- IMD forecasts warn of **above-normal heatwave days** April–June across East, Central, North-West India, and the South-East Peninsula
- Summer 2026 expected to **match or surpass 2024's record** (which saw 50°C+ temperatures, phones and ACs catching fire)
- IMD issued warnings of **"warm nights"** — staying hot even after sunset, a dangerous phenomenon
- Southwest monsoon expected to advance around June 23, potentially bringing some relief

### Impacts
- Health Ministry activated **heatstroke units**, stocking ORS in hospitals
- Hospitals reporting surge in **heat exhaustion and heatstroke patients**
- NHRC issued advisories on heat wave preparedness
- **48°C "killer heat"** warnings issued by the Independent
- Power demand surging across the north
- Agromet advisories warning of impacts on crops

---

## 🇺🇸 UNITED STATES — "Heat Dome for 200 Million"

A massive heat dome is settling over the eastern half of the US in the last week of June, threatening to make the July 4th holiday weekend dangerously hot.

### Key Facts
- **200 million+ Americans** under heat threat from the Midwest to the East Coast
- **Triple-digit temperatures** (100°F / 37.8°C+) forecast across multiple states
- Heat dome expected to last **from late June into early July**
- **Western US** also experiencing above-average temperatures — from West Coast through the Great Basin, Southwest, and Rockies
- National Weather Service warned of a **"prolonged, dangerous heat wave"**
- Heat indexes expected to exceed **100°F** across the Midwest on June 29-30
- El Niño developing, which typically amplifies summer heat

### Impacts
- Record-breaking July 4th temperatures expected in NYC, DC, Philadelphia
- Emergency cooling centers opening across eastern cities
- Energy bills predicted to "soar" coast to coast
- Combined with severe storms and wildfire risk in the West

---

## 🌡️ What's Driving This

- **Persistent heat domes** parked over each region, trapping hot air
- **Sahara-fed air masses** pushing into Europe
- **El Niño developing** — historically amplifies global temperatures
- June 2026 caps the **hottest 12-month period** on record globally
- Climate scientists (Yale Climate Connections, WHO) linking the events to accelerating climate change
