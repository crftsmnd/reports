# Meghalaya News Roundup — June 23, 2026

Curated from India Today NE, Northeast Now, Times of India, and other local outlets.

---

## 🥇 Meghalaya ECD Mission Wins Gold SKOCH Award

Meghalaya's Early Childhood Development (ECD) Mission won the **Gold SKOCH Award** at the 108th SKOCH Summit in New Delhi. The recognition came after a multi-stage national evaluation starting March 2026, including project presentations, public voting, and third-party expert assessments. The mission, led by the Department of Social Welfare, was praised for its coordinated model linking health, education, rural development, and community institutions to improve early childhood outcomes.

## 🎬 Film City, Film Institute & Music Institute Announced

The state government announced plans for a **Film City and Film Institute in Shillong**, and a **Music Institute in Tura**. This is aimed at boosting Meghalaya's creative economy and opening up opportunities for local artists and filmmakers.

## 🎵 Reble Named Icon of the Year at Hello Meghalaya Music Awards

Singer **Reble** was awarded Icon of the Year at the inaugural Hello Meghalaya Music Awards held on World Music Day. CM Conrad Sangma hailed her as an icon, and Reble in turn urged Meghalaya's youth to believe in their talent.

## 🌧️ Heavy Rains & Landslide Disrupt Connectivity

- **Mawsynram** recorded **530 mm of rainfall** in 24 hours — one of the heaviest downpours this season.
- A major **landslide on the Shillong–Dawki Highway** near Laitlyngkot village has shut down the road, disrupting connectivity in East Khasi Hills indefinitely.

## 🧘 Rajnath Singh Marks Yoga Day in Shillong

Defence Minister **Rajnath Singh** joined armed forces personnel in Shillong for International Day of Yoga celebrations, alongside troops across borders, coasts, and naval platforms nationwide.

## 🏗️ Nirmala Sitharaman's Meghalaya Visit — Key Highlights

Union Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman concluded a two-day visit with several announcements:

- **Inaugurated** the Northeast's largest **organic spice processing unit** at Bhoirymbong, Ri-Bhoi district, expected to benefit **5,500 farmers**.
- **Inaugurated** the **₹59-crore Jowai–Nartiang–Kdiap–Khanduli road**.
- **Reviewed** the **Wahiajer Football Stadium** project for youth development.
- **Visited Larnai village** and praised Meghalaya's GI-tagged black clay pottery.
- Announced externally aided project funding in the Northeast has **risen seven-fold** under the Modi government, with **₹1,246 crore** in new projects for Meghalaya.

## 🏛️ KHADC Passes Service Licence Bill for Non-Tribal Employees

The **Khasi Hills Autonomous District Council (KHADC)** passed a bill mandating a service licence for non-tribal employees working in the Khasi Hills region.

## 🔍 GHADC Logo Controversy

A row erupted over the alleged omission of the word **"autonomous"** from the **Garo Hills Autonomous District Council (GHADC)** logo, sparking political debate.

## 🏫 School Controversies in the Spotlight

- An activist accused a **Catholic school principal** of intimidation after filing a student assault complaint.
- A **Class IX student** was allegedly barred from sitting for exams at a school in South Garo Hills after an FIR was filed against a teacher over corporal punishment.

## 🐒 Meghalaya Zoo Breeds Vulnerable Stump-Tailed Macaque

The **Meghalaya State Zoo** recorded its **first successful breeding** of the vulnerable stump-tailed macaque, a milestone for ex-situ conservation.

## 🔬 NIA Gets 45 More Days in Bangladesh Activist Murder Case

A Delhi court granted the NIA **45 more days** to investigate the cross-border killing of Bangladeshi activist Sharif Osman Bin Hadi, with the probe examining his alleged entry through the Meghalaya border.

## 🎓 Other Notable Updates

- Meghalaya signed an **MoU with Singapore's ITEES** to strengthen vocational training and skill development.
- **MPYC** raised concerns over inadequate support for Meghalaya students appearing for the NEET re-examination.
- **USTM** announced scholarships and fellowships for 50 Nagaland students.
- Traffic curbs were in place in Upper Shillong for the NEET re-exam day.
